Consent to be Part of a Research Study

 

Title of the Project:A Review of Reflection Support Tools

Principal Investigator: Daniel Head, UNC Charlotte

Faculty Advisor: Dr. Mindy Adnot, UNC Charlotte

 

You are invited to participate in a research study.Participation in this research study is voluntary.The information provided is to help you decide whether or not to participate.If you have any questions, please ask.

 

Important Information You Need to Know

  • The purpose of this study is to study your use of reflection support tools in the past and how you would rate the effectiveness of a new reflection support tool.
  • You will be asked to complete an online survey answering questions about your past experiences with reflection, briefly use a new reflection support tool online, and return to the survey to answer questions regarding that reflection support tool.
  • If you choose to participate it will require 20 minutes of your time.
  • We do not believe that you will experience any risk from participating in this study.
  • You will not benefit personally by participating in this study. However, what we learn about how people use and rate reflection support tools may be beneficial to others.

 

Please read this form and ask any questions you may have before you decide whether to participate in this research study.

 

Why are we doing this study?

The purpose of this study is to determine if and how reflection support tools can be improved. This is because reflection can be an important process of the student learning process, and we would like to aid this process.

 

Why are you being asked to be in this research study.

You are being asked to be in this study because you are a member of the University Honors Program, where reflection is heavily used within courses and the program itself, and there is a diverse group of majors present.

 

What will happen if I take part in this study?

If you choose to participate in this study, you will be asked to you will be asked to complete an online survey answering questions about your past experiences with reflection, briefly use a new reflection support tool online, and return to the survey to answer questions regarding that reflection support tool. You may complete this online survey, and use this tool, at any time convenient to you within two weeks of receiving the invitation to the survey.

 

You time commitment will be about 20 minutes to complete the survey and use of the new reflection support tool.

 

What benefits might I experience?

You will not benefit personally by participating in this study.However, what we learn about how people use and rate reflection support tools may be beneficial to others.

 

What risks might I experience?

We do not believe that you will experience any risk from participating in this study.

 

How will my information be protected?

Your privacy will be protected and confidentiality will be maintained to the extent possible. Your responses will be treated as confidential but will be linked to your identity.  You are being asked to provide your name and email address.  We need your email address in order to enter you into a drawing for and/or send you the e-gift card.Also, Incentive payments are considered taxable income.Therefore, we are required to give the University's Financial Services division a log/tracking sheet with the names of all individuals who received a gift card.This sheet is for tax purposes only and is separate from the research data, which means the names on this sheet will not be linked to survey responses.

 

We plan to publish the results of this study. To protect your privacy we will not include any information that could identify you.We will protect the confidentiality of the research data by deleting personal identifiers to you.

 

Survey responses and identifiers will be stored together with access to this information controlled and limited only to people who have approval to have access. After we complete the drawing, your identifiers will be deleted.We might use the survey data for future research studies and we might share the non-identifiable survey data with other researchers for future research studies without additional consent from you.

 

Other people may need to see the information we collect about you. Including people who work for UNC Charlotte, and other agencies as required by law or allowed by federal regulations.

 

Will I receive an incentive for taking part in this study?

You will be included in a drawing for one of three, $10 Starbucks gift cards at the completion of participation.

 

Who can profit from this study?

The study Investigator developed the web application being used in this study.This means the Investigator may gain financially.

 

What are my rights if I take part in this study?

It is up to you to decide to be in this research study. Participating in this study is voluntary. Even if you decide to be part of the study now, you may change your mind and stop at any time. You do not have to answer any questions you do not want to answer.

 

If you withdraw, your information will be deleted and you will not be entered in the drawing.

 

Who can answer my questions about this study and my rights as a participant?

For questions about this research, you may contact Principal Investigator Daniel Head, by email at dhead3@uncc.edu, and Faculty Advisor Dr. Mindy Adnot, by email at madnot@uncc.edu.

 

If you have questions about your rights as a research participant, or wish to obtain information, ask questions, or discuss any concerns about this study with someone other than the researcher(s), please contact the Office of Research Compliance at 704-687-1871 or uncc-irb@uncc.edu.
